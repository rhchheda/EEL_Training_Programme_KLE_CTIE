The 5 Safety Rules

1
Never work on a live circuit — always de-energise first.
Switch off and verify power is off before touching any component.
2
Always wear goggles and gloves when working with live components or soldering.
No exceptions — not for “just a second”.
3
Never touch circuit components with wet hands.
Water and electricity is a medical emergency, not just a shock.
4
If you are unsure — stop and ask. Never guess.
There are no stupid questions here. Guessing is the only wrong answer.
5
Report damage, sparks, or unusual smells immediately.
Stop work, move people back, inform the instructor at once.
THESE RULES APPLY FROM THIS MOMENT — IN THIS LAB — EVERY SINGLE SESSION

LAB INVENTORY

Meet Your Equipment

These items are on your workbench right now. Pick each one up as it is discussed.

🔌
Plug Top
WHAT IT IS
The connector at the end of a power cable. Contains Live (brown), Neutral (blue), and Earth (green/yellow) connections.
Day 4: You will fully disassemble and reassemble one of these.
⚡
MCB / Fuse
WHAT IT IS
A Miniature Circuit Breaker automatically cuts power when current exceeds a safe limit. A fuse does the same — but burns out and must be replaced.
Not for repair today — just for recognition and understanding.
🔍
Digital Multimeter
WHAT IT IS
Your most important tool. Measures Voltage, Current, Resistance, and tests Continuity. Think of it as a stethoscope for circuits.
Day 5: You will use this independently on a live circuit.

V
Voltage — The Pressure
Think of it as water pressure in a tank. The higher the voltage, the harder it pushes electricity through a circuit.
I
Current — The Flow
The actual amount of electricity flowing. Like water through a pipe — measured in Amperes (A).
R
Resistance — The Friction
How much the circuit resists the flow. A narrow pipe slows water down. A resistor does the same for electricity.

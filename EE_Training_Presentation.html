ABOUT THE PROGRAMME

What is
this about?

Designed for professionals with zero electronics background — startup founders, pharma, biotech, mechanical, and management professionals who need practical electrical skills for their work environment.

10SESSIONS
2WEEKS
6–10PER BATCH
🎯

Who Is It For?

Non-electronics professionals — startup founders, pharma, biotech, mechanical, management, or any domain where basic electrical knowledge adds value.

⚡

What Will You Learn?

Electrical safety, circuit basics, multimeter use, plug tops & sockets, soldering, fault diagnosis, desoldering & rework, and real-world scenario simulations.

🏅

What Do You Get?

Hands-on experience with EE Lab inventory, a practical assessment, and a Certificate of Completion from KLE-CTIE upon clearing the threshold score.

MATERIALS REQUIRED

Lab Materials & Equipment Checklist

All items to be made available in EE Lab before Day 1

⚡ Electrical Basics

  • Plug tops (2-pin & 3-pin)
  • Extension boards / sockets
  • Assorted wires (red/black/green)
  • Wire strippers
  • Screwdrivers (flat & Phillips)
  • Insulation tape / heat shrink
  • MCB / fuse samples

🔬 Measurement Tools

  • Digital multimeters (1 per 2 participants)
  • Probes & test leads
  • Battery holders & 9V batteries
  • DC power supply unit
  • Breadboards
  • Jumper wires
  • Resistor kit (various values)

🔥 Soldering Station

  • Soldering irons (1 per participant)
  • Solder wire (60/40 rosin core)
  • Flux paste / liquid flux
  • Desoldering wick & pump
  • PCB practice boards
  • Through-hole components
  • Tip cleaner / wet sponge

🧩 Components

  • Resistors (variety pack)
  • Capacitors (electrolytic & ceramic)
  • LEDs (mixed colours)
  • Switches (toggle & push)
  • Relays & transistors
  • Diodes (1N4007)
  • Component storage trays

🛡️ Safety & PPE

  • Safety goggles (1 per participant)
  • Rubber gloves (insulated)
  • Anti-static wrist straps
  • Fire extinguisher (nearby)
  • First aid kit
  • Fume extractor / ventilation
  • Safety signage

📋 Lab & Admin

  • Printed reference sheets (Day 1)
  • Lab notebooks / log sheets
  • Projector / display screen
  • Whiteboard & markers
  • Attendance register
  • Assessment answer sheets
  • Certificate print paper (if manual)
⚠ All equipment must be inspected & tested before Day 1 · Spare consumables (solder, PCBs, components) recommended at 2× quantity

PROGRAMME DESIGN

Core Design Principles

The philosophy behind how this programme is structured and delivered

🛠️
Hands-On First

Minimal theory — maximum doing. Every session ends with a practical task using real lab equipment.

🛡️
Safety Embedded

Safety is not a slide — it is the foundation. Reinforced at the start of every single session throughout the programme.

🧩
No Prior Knowledge

Designed from zero — accessible to anyone regardless of educational or professional background.

🎯
Outcome-Focused

Competencies are defined upfront. Participants leave with real, usable, and safe electrical skills.

👥
Small Batch: 6–10

Intentionally small cohorts to ensure quality lab time, individual mentorship, and hands-on equipment access.

🌱
Ecosystem-Driven

Rooted in KLE-CTIE Startup Street — connecting learning directly with real entrepreneurial and operational needs.

🏅

Build Confidence.
Build Competence.

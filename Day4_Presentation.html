WHY THIS SESSION EXISTS

Most electrical
fires start at
the plug.

Loose connections, incorrect wiring, wrong fuse ratings, and damaged cables are responsible for the majority of electrical incidents in labs and offices. Every one of them is preventable with basic knowledge.

40% OF ELECTRICAL FIRES
START AT CONNECTIONS
3 min TO SAFELY REPLACE
A PLUG TOP
🔥
Loose terminal screws
A poorly tightened screw creates resistance at the connection. That resistance generates heat. Over time, heat causes arcing — a small electrical fire inside the plug.
⚠
Wrong fuse rating
A 13A fuse in a lamp circuit designed for 3A will not blow when it should. The cable overheats first. Fitting the correct fuse is the simplest safety act in this entire programme.
🧵
Reversed Live and Neutral
A plug wired with Live and Neutral swapped will still power the device. But the switch in the appliance now disconnects the wrong wire — leaving the device energised even when switched off.
❌
Missing Earth connection
If the metal casing of an appliance becomes live due to an internal fault, the Earth wire carries that current safely to ground. Without it, the current goes through the person who touches the casing.
ANATOMY

Every Part
of a Plug Top

Pick up your plug top. Find each part as it is named.

Earth Pin (E)
The longest pin. Top-centre position. Green and yellow wire. Longest because it connects first on insertion and disconnects last on removal.
Live Pin (L)
Bottom-left. Brown wire. The dangerous one — carries the active 230V supply. The pin you must never touch on a connected plug.
Neutral Pin (N)
Bottom-right. Blue wire. Completes the circuit back to the supply. Theoretically at near-zero voltage but still dangerous — never assumed safe.
Fuse
Sits in a holder across the Live circuit. Rated 3A or 13A. Blows when current exceeds the safe limit, breaking the circuit before damage occurs.
Cable Grip
The plastic bar that clamps the outer cable sheath. Prevents mechanical force on the cable from being transmitted to the terminal screws.
Terminal Screws
One at each pin. Must be tightened firmly. A loose terminal is the most common cause of plug overheating and electrical fires.

ENGINEERING STORY

Why the Earth Pin
is Longest

This is not a random design choice. The length difference between the Earth pin and the Live/Neutral pins is a deliberate safety engineering decision that protects millions of people every day.

1
Plug is inserted into socket
The Earth pin (longest) enters the socket first. This makes the Earth connection before any power can flow.
2
Earth shutter mechanism releases
In Indian sockets, the Earth pin pushes open the safety shutters that block the Live and Neutral holes, preventing insertion of foreign objects.
3
Live and Neutral connect
Only after Earth is connected do the Live and Neutral pins enter the socket. The appliance is now safely earthed before any current flows.
4
Removal reverses the sequence
Live and Neutral disconnect first. Earth remains connected until last — ensuring the appliance casing stays grounded during the transition.
WHAT THE EARTH WIRE ACTUALLY DOES
Imagine the Live wire inside a metal kettle frays and touches the metal body. Without Earth: you pick up the kettle and 230V passes through your body to ground. With Earth: the current flows down the Earth wire, trips the circuit breaker, and the kettle simply stops working. The Earth wire saved your life.
WITHOUT EARTH — THE RISK
An unearthed appliance with an internal fault presents its full mains voltage on the casing. A person touching it completes the circuit to ground through their body. This is how electrocution occurs in domestic settings.
THE FUSE

The Fuse
Inside the Plug

The fuse is a thin wire inside a glass or ceramic cartridge. When current exceeds the rated value, the wire melts and breaks the circuit. It is a sacrificial component — designed to fail so the appliance and wiring do not.

COMMON FUSE RATINGS
1A
Very small loads. Shavers, phone chargers, LED lamps. Rare in standard plug tops.
3A
Most lamps, small appliances under 700W. Computers, phone chargers, desk fans. Use this as the default for low-power devices.
5A
Medium appliances. Some monitors, small heaters. Used when 3A is too low but 13A is excessive.
13A
High-power appliances. Kettles, toasters, irons, washing machines. Max for standard UK/Indian plug tops.
THE GOLDEN RULE
If a fuse blows, find out why before replacing it. A fuse that blows repeatedly is the circuit telling you something is wrong. Fit a higher-rated fuse to stop it blowing and you remove the only protection the appliance has.
QUICK FORMULA
Fuse rating ≈ Power (W) ÷ Voltage (V)
A 700W appliance at 230V needs: 700÷230 = 3A fuse. Always round up to the next standard size.
HANDS-ON — DISASSEMBLY

Taking It Apart

BEFORE YOU TOUCH ANYTHING
Confirm your plug top is completely disconnected. No cable attached. No socket in reach. Place it flat on the bench. Only then pick up the screwdriver.
1
Identify the central screw on the face of the plug. This is the main body screw. Use a flat-head screwdriver.
2
Unscrew the central screw fully. Keep it safe — place it in the component tray. Do not lose it.
3
Gently separate the two halves of the plug body. The top cover lifts away from the base.
4
Locate the fuse between the Live terminal and the Live pin. Note its rating. Remove and set aside.
5
Examine the three terminal screws. Each should have a wire connection point. Note which colour wire goes where.
6
Identify the cable grip. Note how it clamps the outer sheath of the cable.

HANDS-ON — WIRING

Wiring It
Correctly

The instructor will wire a plug top at the front first. Watch every step. Then complete your own. Do not proceed past Step 3 until the instructor has checked your wire colours.

Live (Brown) → L terminal (bottom-left)
Strip 8mm of insulation. Wrap clockwise around screw. Tighten firmly — no bare wire visible outside the terminal.
Neutral (Blue) → N terminal (bottom-right)
Same stripping and tightening procedure. Confirm Blue goes to N — never to L.
Earth (Green/Yellow) → E terminal (top)
Longest wire. Connects to the largest terminal. Tighten firmly. The Earth must never be loose.
Fit the fuse
Select the correct rating for the appliance. Click the fuse cartridge into its holder between the L terminal and the Live pin.
Secure the cable grip & close
Tighten the cable grip screw over the outer sheath. Close the plug body. Tighten the central screw firmly.
INSTRUCTOR CHECK BEFORE CLOSING
✓Brown wire in L terminal only
✓Blue wire in N terminal only
✓Green/Yellow wire in E terminal
✓No bare wire visible outside terminals
✓All terminal screws tightened
✓Correct fuse rating fitted
✓Cable grip tightened over sheath
NEVER DO THIS
Never omit the Earth wire. Never swap Live and Neutral. Never use a higher-rated fuse to stop it blowing. Never leave bare wire exposed outside a terminal. These four errors cause fires and fatalities.
FAULT SPOTTING EXERCISE

Spot the Danger

Each card describes a plug top scenario. Identify the fault and the risk. Click to reveal the answer.

SCENARIO 01
Warm plug after 1 hour
A plug top feels noticeably warm to the touch after the appliance has been running for one hour. The fuse has not blown.
Risk: Intermittent or progressive fault
Likely cause: Loose terminal screw creating resistance. The resistance generates heat without being high enough to blow the fuse. Unplug immediately. Open and re-tighten all terminal screws.

SCENARIO 02
Fuse blows on every connection
A 3A fuse in a desk lamp blows every time the lamp is switched on. Someone suggests replacing it with a 13A fuse to solve the problem.
Risk: Masking a real fault
Never do this. The fuse is blowing because the current exceeds 3A — this is a fault in the lamp or wiring. Fitting a 13A fuse removes the protection and allows the fault to persist. Diagnose the lamp first.
CLICK TO REVEAL
SCENARIO 03
Brown wire at N terminal
During inspection, you find a plug with the Brown wire connected to the N terminal and the Blue wire connected to the L terminal.
Risk: Reversed polarity
This is a wiring error — Live and Neutral are reversed. The appliance may work normally BUT the switch in the device now disconnects the Neutral, leaving the Live wire permanently connected to the appliance internals even when switched off.
CLICK TO REVEAL
SCENARIO 04
No Earth wire connected
You open a plug top and find only two wires — Brown (L) and Blue (N). The E terminal is empty. The appliance is a metal-bodied drill.
Risk: Lethal if internal fault occurs
Metal-bodied power tools must be earthed. Without the Earth wire, any internal fault that puts voltage on the metal body will go through the user to ground. Replace the cable with a 3-core cable and connect the Earth before any use.
CLICK TO REVEAL
SCENARIO 05
Bare copper visible at terminal
After wiring, a small length of bare copper conductor is visible outside the terminal screw clamp on the Live wire.
Risk: Contact with live conductor
Any bare conductor outside the terminal is a shock hazard. When the plug closes, the bare wire could contact the plug body or another terminal. Strip only 8mm of insulation. If too much is stripped, re-strip from a point further back on the cable.
CLICK TO REVEAL
SCENARIO 06
Cable grip over wire insulation
The cable grip screw is tightened over the individual wire insulation rather than the outer cable sheath. The outer sheath ends before the grip.
Risk: Mechanical stress on terminals
The cable grip must clamp the outer sheath — the tough outer jacket of the cable. If it clamps individual wires, any pull on the cable is transmitted directly to the terminal screws, which will loosen over time and cause the plug to overheat.

SOCKET INSPECTION

Checking
Sockets &
Extension Boards

Plug tops are only half the connection. The socket or extension board matters equally. These are the visual checks you can perform safely without any tools.

👁
Discolouration or scorch marks
Brown or black marks around a socket face indicate arcing. Remove from service immediately — do not use.
🤚
Mechanical looseness
A socket that moves, wobbles, or feels loose in the wall should be flagged for repair. A loose socket can arc intermittently without showing visible signs.
🔥
Warm face plate
A socket face plate that feels warm to touch even with nothing plugged in indicates a wiring fault behind the plate. Do not use — report immediately.
🔌
Extension board overloading
Never connect high-power appliances (kettles, heaters, air conditioners) to standard extension boards. Check the total wattage of all connected devices against the board rating.
NEVER DO THESE
—Daisy-chain extension boards (plug one into another)
—Use a socket with visible scorch marks
—Overload a single socket with multiple high-power devices
—Use a damaged or cracked socket face plate
—Block ventilation around extension boards
—Run extension leads under carpets or through doorways
